What are Microplastics?

    • Microbeads

      The tiny beeds in your tooth paste are actualy plastic and they go down your drain into the sewage system.

    • Fleeces

      Fleeces and jackets have little strands of fur that fall off and fly into the air. A single fleece can shed as many as 1,900 fibers in a single wash!

    • Car Tires

      Close to 2 billion tyres are produced annually around the globe. Plastic tyre dust is washed away into the our lakes and seas.

    • Manufacturing

      While manufacturing plastic they use these small resin pellets and when being transfered by sea and can be spilled into the ocean without knowing

  • 1,500,000Tonnes Per Year

    Microplastics are very small pieces of plastic that pollute the environment. Microplastics are not a specific kind of plastic, but rather any type of plastic fragment that is less than five millimeters in length.

Some of the foods we eat

  • Salt

    The average adult consumes approximately 2,000 microplastics per year through salt. Lake and sea salts from eight countries sampled all contained microplastics; results ranged from 0 to 10 particles per kg salt.

  • Fish

    It is estimated that people could be unknowingly ingesting 11,000 microfibers each year from shellfish consumption, and 178 microfibers from eating a single mussel

  • Honey

    All 19 samples of honey from four different countries (France, Italy, Spain and Mexico) contained plastic particulates; results ranged from 40 to 660 plastic fibers per kg honey.

  • Beer

    It appears nothing is safe from plastic pollution anymore - not even the beer we drink. A recent study published in the Public Library of Science's journal found that on average, beer contains 4.05 man-made particles, mostly plastic fibers, per liter. If the results are generalized across all beers, researchers estimates that people drinking an average of a beer a day will consume 520 particles annually.

What we breath

  • Home

    Plastic microfibers found in the dust in our homes and the air we breathe can come from car tyres, carpets and soft furnishings, as well as clothes such as fleece jackets. These are regularly shedding tiny bits of plastic into the environment as they are worn away. It was found that humans likely consume about 114 plastic microfibers each meal simply from household dust that settles on their plates.

  • Mountains

    A study took place in a remote spot four miles from the nearest village and roughly 75 miles from the nearest city, Toulouse. Researchers, taking samples from two separate monitoring devices, found that 365 pieces of microplastic per square meter rained down from the sky each day
